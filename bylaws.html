KRIPA Constitution and Bylaws

PREAMBLE

THE KNOXVILLE REGION INDIAN ORIGIN PHYSICIANS ASSOCIATION (KRIPA) was formed to address a need for cohesive action on the part of physicians, dentists and other medical scientists of Indian origin, residing in Knoxville and neighboring areas. The KRIPA was also formed to maintain the identity of this group of people while providing a forum for scientific, educational, and charitable purpose among its members.

The term “Indian” is used throughout these By-laws in an ethnic sense and is meant exclusively to pertain to the Indian subcontinent of Asia. The word “heritage” similarly, connotes ancestry.

ARTICLE I –

OBJECTIVES

The objectives of the KRIPA are, in addition to and not in substitution of those stated in the KRIPA’s Articles of Incorporation, the following:

A. To bring together individuals and organizations of physicians, dentists and other medical scientists of Indian heritage practicing in the KNOXVILLE region under one umbrella organization.
B. The KRIPA is organized exclusively for scientific, educational, and charitable purpose under section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, or corresponding section of any future federal tax code. Upon the dissolution of the organization, assets shall be distributed for one or more exempt purposes within the meaning of section 501 (c) (3) of the Internal Revenue Code.
C. To conduct seminars and other educational activities to inform members of new scientific developments in the field of human medicine.
D. To make contributions to organizations that qualify as exempt organizations under Section 501(c) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 as amended, or the corresponding provision of any succeeding section or future United States Internal Revenue Code Section.
E. To provide mutual understanding and cooperation between the KRIPA and other medical society in the United States, namely AMERICAN ASSOCIATION OF PHYSICIANS OF INDIAN ORIGIN (AAPI) of which KRIPA happens to be a sister organization.
F. To maintain a directory of Knoxville region physicians of Indian origin.
G. To provide other member services as needed.

ARTICLE II – GOVERNING BODY

The KRIPA’s governing body would consist of elected officials by majority vote. These elected members would be serving purely on a volunteer basis with no compensation and the term would be of one year duration. It would include the President, the Vice-President, the Treasurer, and the Secretary/Information-Technology (IT) Master. There would be no paid members or staff in the KRIPA organization.

ARTICLE III– MEMBERSHIP

Section 3.1 – General

Membership in the KRIPA shall be open to any Physician or Dentist of Indian Origin and other physicians in the Knoxville region who wish to promote the mission of the KRIPA and make continuing contributions towards the goals of KRIPA, or organizations representing or comprised of physicians, dentists or scientists in the field of the human sciences, and who are of Indian heritage in the United States. Membership shall also be open to any student, student group or organization made up of students of Indian heritage and other students in the Knoxville region who wish to promote the mission of the KRIPA and make continuing contributions towards the goals of KRIPA, studying or training in the Knoxville region, in the area of the human sciences.

Section 3.2 - Categories of Membership Members shall be divided into the following categories:

A. ACTIVE MEMBERS: Active members shall be annual members who have paid their membership dues for previous year, and for current year.
B. MEDICAL STUDENTS, RESIDENTS AND FELLOWS (MSRF): Residents, Fellows and Students of medicine and dentistry, in approved training programs in the United States, who wish to promote the mission of the KRIPA will be represented in the KRIPA through the KRIPA’s Medical Student/Resident section. Their participation will be governed in accordance with the By-laws of the KRIPA, which will conform to the broad principles of the KRIPA and as approved by the General and the Governing Body.
C. SPONSOR MEMBERS: Industrial partners of KRIPA who sponsor programs and activities of KRIPA and satisfy criteria as set by the Governing body from time to time may be admitted as sponsor members for the duration of their sponsorship. Sponsor members are not entitled to vote or hold any elected office, but may be appointed to Committees for their professional expertise to serve KRIPA.
D. OTHER MEMBERS: The Governing Body may from time to time create other special categories of membership.

Section 3.3 - Member Qualifications

Any person or an association of persons as set forth in Section 3.2, may attain membership in the KRIPA provided that the applicant:
A. Subscribes to the objectives of the KRIPA, abides by the By-laws, rules and regulations and pays the prescribed fees and dues.
B. Receives the approval of its application for membership by a majority vote of the Governing Body after due screening.

Section 3.4 – Impeachment

A. If any member to an elected or appointed office or position misuses their office or are guilty of misconduct or violate the objectives of the KRIPA’s Article of Incorporation or By-laws, for personal gain or misappropriate the funds of the KRIPA or violate the law resulting in civil liability or a criminal offense and conviction, he or she shall be liable for impeachment or forfeiture of his or her position, immediately. Further, the Governing Body is authorized to remove such a person temporarily from the position held in the KRIPA, pending the final outcome of the judicial proceeding.
B. An impeachment procedure may be initiated by a petition signed by at least two-thirds of the Governing Body.
C. After an impeachment procedure is initiated as provided in these By-laws, then the Governing Body shall appoint a hearing committee to investigate the allegation and recommend necessary action to the Governing Body. The hearing committee shall follow a mechanism adopted by the Governing Body and consistently applied.
D. An impeachment can be effected only upon the affirmative vote of at least two-thirds of the Governing Body.

ARTICLE IV - ANNUAL CONVENTION



Section 4.1 - Purpose

The Annual Meeting of the General Body shall be held at the time designated by the Governing Body, provided however, that the Governing Body shall call for a meeting of the General Body not less than annually. The purpose of the Annual Convention is to bring together all the members once a year and to discuss issues of interest to physicians of Indian heritage in the Knoxville region.

Section 4.2 – Organization

The current KRIPA Governing body would initiate the election process of incoming new Governing body officials by majority vote cast by all the KRIPA members at the annual meeting.

ARTICLE V - ORGANIZATION OF THE CORPORATE BODY



Section 5.1 - Components of the Corporate Body

The KRIPA shall be made up of all the members (also known as the General Body), and the Governing Body.

Section 5.2 - General Body

Subject to the KRIPA’s Articles of Incorporation and these By-laws, the General Body retains the ultimate authority of the KRIPA.

A. The General Body shall consist of voting members as set forth in paragraph D of this section.
B. The General Body meetings will be posted on the KRIPA Website and voting members shall be notified via mail, email or fax at least 30 days before each General Body meeting. It shall be the responsibility of the voting members to keep their email and postal address current at the KRIPA website.
C. General Body meeting shall be open to all the voting members.
D. Voting members of the General Body shall be determined as per section 8.1
E. At the Annual General Body Meeting, those members present and eligible to vote may conduct the business of the General Body. If a member eligible to vote makes a quorum call, the Presiding Officer shall decide if a quorum is present.

i. Twenty percent (20%) of the voting members registered at the annual convention constitute a quorum for that meeting of the General Body. Once a quorum is established, the business of the meeting shall continue and all actions at the meeting shall be binding, even if a quorum is not present at later times in the meeting.
ii. If, upon a quorum call, a quorum is not present as defined above, then the Presiding Office may entertain motions to recess, adjourn, or adjourn to reconvene at a later date. (i.e., fix the time to which to adjourn). In addition, the Presiding Officer may arrange for a mail-in ballot to decide specific and urgent issues scheduled for consideration at that meeting. A simple majority of the returned ballots shall decide the issues, so long as not otherwise required by the Articles of Incorporation or the laws governing such matters.
iii. In case of an important issue which needs immediate attention of the General Body, the Governing Body may authorize decide to arrange for a mail-in ballot to decide specific and urgent issues, which can not wait till the next General Body Meeting. A simple majority of the returned ballots shall decide the issues, so long as not otherwise required by the Articles of Incorporation or the laws governing such matters.

Section 5.3 - Governing Body

The Governing Body is the main deliberative body of the KRIPA for policy creation and acts on behalf of the General Body. Only those members of the Governing Body who are also Voting Members of the General Body as defined under Section 5.2 D may be eligible to vote at the Governing Body.

A. The Governing Body shall be composed of the President, the vice-President, the Treasurer and the Secretary/IT Master official.
B. The Governing Body shall direct and execute the policies of the Association. It may fill any vacancies in the Governing Body by appointment, in accordance with the provisions of the Articles of Incorporation and these By-laws. It shall create all standing committees and set policy for their activities. The Governing Body shall set the membership dues for all categories of membership.
C. The Governing Body shall meet not less than three (3) times per year in regular session. One such meeting shall occur during the Annual Convention and such meeting shall constitute the Annual Meeting of the Governing Body.
D. A quorum for any meeting of the Governing Body shall consist of two-thirds of the Governing Body members registered at the respective meeting.
E. Contracts, deeds, documents and instruments shall be executed by the President and attested by the Secretary or the Treasurer.
F. Checks, notes, drafts and demands for money shall be signed by the Treasurer.
G Onely active members of the KRIPA within the membership of the KRIPA as described by Article II shall be eligible for nomination and election to these offices.

ARTICLE VI - DUTIES OF OFFICERS


No elected officer shall serve more than two consecutive terms in the same office. A term of office will be one year.

Section 6.1 – President

a. The President shall be the Chief Executive Officer of the KRIPA and shall perform all duties incident to the office of the President and the Governing Body may assign such other duties, as it may deem proper, necessary or useful.
b. He or she shall preside at all meetings of the KRIPA including of the Governing Body.
c. He or she shall make such appointments as are required or authorized by the Articles of Incorporation, these By-laws and by the Governing Body.
d. He or she may execute, with the majority of the Governing Body members, any deeds, mortgages, bonds, contracts or other instruments which the Governing Body has authorized to be executed.
e. The President and members of the Governing Body will be jointly responsible to the General Body. In the event of any disagreements in the execution of the policies of the KRIPA, the President shall make the final decision, which shall be binding on all parties concerned. The President, however, shall be required to validate his or her decision either before or as soon as possible after the event by majority vote of the General Body. If the President fails to accomplish such validation, the majority decision of the General Body shall prevail. Prior approval by the Governing Body is mandatory for any decision involving financial transactions on behalf of the KRIPA, which may result in significant financial consequences for the KRIPA.

Section 6.2 - Vice President

a. The Vice President shall assist the President, Treasurer and IT Master as needed at the direction of the President.
b. The Vice President shall serve as the Chair of the Membership Committee.


Section 6.3 – Secretary/IT Master

The Secretary/IT Master shall:

a. Prepare & maintain the minutes of all meetings;
b. Maintain an updated list of all members of the KRIPA on its website.
c. Issue an agenda of all regular and special meetings after consultation with the Governing Body.
d. Keep a copy of all official correspondence from and to the KRIPA.
e. Keep an attendance record of all meetings of the KRIPA.
f. Be custodian of the Articles of Incorporation, By-laws and amendments thereto;
g. See that all books, reports, statements, certificates and other documents and records required by law to be kept and filed are properly kept and filed,

Section 6.4 – Treasurer

Unless otherwise directed by the Governing Body, the Treasurer will be the sole fiscal officer and the only authorized person empowered to have custody of the financial accounts of the KRIPA, subject to audit powers of the Governing Body, the General Body and any outside auditors the KRIPA may select.

The Treasurer shall:

a. Be responsible for issuing notice and collection of all dues and deposit the same in such banks. He or she will be in charge of all the accounts of the KRIPA;
b. Have custody of all accounts, receipts and disbursements. All Accounts shall be open at all reasonable times to inspection by the Governing and the General Body;
c. Submit a financial report to the General Body at its meeting at least once in six months. If the Governing Body requires he or she shall give a bond with such surety and in such amount as the Governing Body may designate;
d. Perform such other duties and exercise such other powers and duties incident to the office of the Treasurer and as may be assigned by the President or the Governing Body or otherwise as may be required by law;
e. Transfer to the next Treasurer within 30 days of the assumption of office by the latter, all accounts of the KRIPA;
f. File all income tax returns with the Internal Revenue Service for the year in which the office was held.

ARTICLE VII– DUES



Section 7.1 - Determination of Annual Dues

Annual dues for all categories shall be determined by the Governing Body and approved by the General Body during the Annual meeting.

Section 7.2 – Exemptions

Medical Student/Resident/Fellow members shall not be required to pay dues.

ARTICLE VIII– ELECTIONS



Section 8.1: Eligibility Criteria for the Voters in KRIPA Elections:

i. All paid Annual Members.
ii. One vote per member.


Section 8.2: Call for Nominations

A. The Nomination Committee shall invite nominations from the entire voting membership via any mode such as postal-mail, e-mail, faxes, telephone.
B. Any current dues paying member of KRIPA can make nominations on behalf of a candidate to the Governing Body member election. Nominations must be seconded by another member. Candidate shall include a written statement of acceptance and also to serve the office if elected.
C. A candidate may file nomination and contest for only one position in any given election. Members are prohibited from nominating more than one candidate for any given position.

Section 8.3 - The Election Process

A. The voting members as provided in Section 10.1 shall have the right to vote. No proxy vote is permitted. Each member shall have one vote for each vacant office.
B. The elections shall be conducted by a majority vote at the annual General Body meeting.
C. In the event that there is no eligible nominee for a vacant position, the President Elect shall ask the members of the General Body to propose nominations for the position and the members select a candidate from these nominations by majority vote and the President Elect shall appoint that candidate to the position.

ARTICLE IX- AMENDMENTS AND ADOPTION



Section 9.1 – Amendments

These Bylaws may be amended, repealed or altered in whole or in part by a 2/3 majority vote of any duly organized meeting of the General Body or by simple majority of mail in ballot.

A. The proposed amendment may be initiated in writing in any General Body or Governing Body meeting with the concurrence of at least two-third members.
B. The proposed amendment, duly initiated, shall be considered and later approved by the two-third majority of the General Body members. On approval by the General Body, the amendments shall be incorporated into these Bylaws with immediate effect
C. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary provided in these By-laws, in the event of a conflict between any provision of the KRIPA’s Articles of Incorporation and these By-laws, the provision contained in the Articles of Incorporation shall be given precedence and priority over the conflicting provision in these By-laws.

These By-laws have been duly adopted in accordance with the Articles of Incorporation and the applicable laws.
